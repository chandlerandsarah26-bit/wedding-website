How it all began

Our Story

"We were just two kids who found each other, lost each other, and found each other again, every single time."

They were thirteen when they met, fast friends almost immediately, the kind of easy that doesn't need explaining. What started as friendship quietly deepened into something harder to name: an obsession with each other's company, a magnetic pull that neither of them quite knew what to do with. They moved through middle school and high school side by side, inseparable in the way that only teenagers can be, until life did what life always does, and pulled them in their own directions.

They didn't really talk for years. But they shared a birthday, and almost every year, one of them would reach out, a quick "happy birthday," a tossup each time over who would say it first. It was a small thread, but it held.

In those years apart, they each accumulated a life: places traveled, companies worked for, people met, goals chased down. Around Christmas one year, Sarah texted Chandler and asked if he was around. He said yes. She asked if he was busy. He said no. And just like that, he took her on a full day in Philadelphia, all of his favorite places, one after another, that stretched long into the night, full of laughter and traded stories.

A few months later, around Easter, the conversation started the same way. Sarah reached out. He was around. He wasn't busy. This time, Sarah was clear: she wasn't up for a trip to Philly. So instead they built a fire, drank Mexican moonshine, and laughed for hours. As it turned out, they had both independently moved back to their parents' houses within weeks of each other, which meant that most of their time was now spent at Sarah's, especially when her parents were off traveling the world. Lots of cooking, wine, and music.

They went to Electric Forest together and fell even more in love watching the sunrise every morning. Then Sarah left for Europe, three months, solo. They reluctantly said goodbye. Within weeks, they were already talking about flying Chandler out to meet her. Sarah sponsored his trip to Portugal, and they had adventures all over, fueled by really good wine, beautiful strangeness, music, and laughter. They said goodbye again. Sarah kept going. Chandler flew home.

When she got back, they spent months living between each other's houses, and for a stretch even got an apartment in Philadelphia together, playing house, figuring out what they were. Chandler was building a business. Sarah was working as a server. They talked about moving somewhere together but kept circling the question without landing. After visiting friends in Colorado, they each, independently, came to the same quiet conclusion: Denver felt right.

Each of them thought they were pushing too hard for it. Each of them thought the other wasn't really interested. Then they had the conversation where that finally came out, and within a month, they had packed up and moved to Denver.

Since September 2024 they have lived in their house in Denver, Colorado near Washington Park, less than five minutes from Chandler's sisters. They go to concerts and music festivals, take long drives up into the mountains, and travel whenever they can. The thread held. It always did.

Travel & Stay

Branches is nestled in Shandaken, New York, deep in the Catskill Mountains, roughly three hours from Philadelphia and the surrounding area. Everything you need for the weekend is right there on property.

Pack for the Catskills in September
Days will be crisp and beautiful — highs in the mid-60s°F. But once the sun goes down, temperatures in the mountains drop quickly, often into the low 40s°F overnight. Bring real layers: a warm jacket, a cozy sweater or flannel, and something heavier if you run cold. You'll be outside for parts of each evening, and you'll be glad you came prepared.
